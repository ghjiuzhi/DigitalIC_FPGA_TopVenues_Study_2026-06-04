LLC Resonant Converter With Reconfigurable
Secondary-Side for Output Voltage Regulation
Samuel S. Queiroz , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, and Levy F. Costa , Member, IEEE
Abstract—This brief proposes a reconfigurable LLC resonant
converter that enables the regulation of a tailored portion of the
output voltage through a novel adaptive transformer turns ratio
scheme implemented on the secondary rectifier. The converter
employs a center-tapped transformer and a switch to reconfigure
the secondary rectifier and adjust the effective turns ratio. Using
the proposed concept, voltage regulation is achieved with a
reduced number of switches and devices in the current’s path, no
modification to the primary side, and fixed-frequency operation.
A laboratory prototype rated at 4 kW with a 1 kV input
voltage is implemented and tested to validate the feasibility and
performance of the proposed converter.
Index Terms—DC, transformer, LLC resonant converter, out-
put voltage regulation, reconfigurable rectifier.
I. INTRODUCTION
THE LLC resonant DC/DC converter is widely used
in several applications due to its high efficiency, soft-
switching capability, and simple implementation [1], [2]. It
has become a popular choice for solid-state transformer (SST)
and electric vehicles (EVs) charging applications [3], [4],
especially when operating as a DC transformer, i.e., constant
voltage gain and output voltage [5], [6]. In this operating
mode, the converter achieves high efficiency, as demonstrated
in [7], but it inherently lacks output voltage regulation.
In SSTs, only limited output regulation (around 5-20%) is
typically required to compensate for potential input voltage
sags, whereas EV charging stations require a wide voltage
range (e.g., 400–800 V). To regulate the output voltage of
the LLC resonant converter as a DC transformer, different
techniques have been discussed in the literature, including
modulation strategies and topological reconfiguration.
Modulation-based approaches, such as phase-shift mod-
ulation and pulse-width modulation (PWM) [8], [9], [10],
will increase switching losses, which significantly reduce effi-
ciency, mainly in high-power applications, which is not desired
in SST systems. Another approach is the topological recon-
figuration techniques, which are based on the modification
of the circuit configuration on the primary and/or secondary
side to achieve output voltage adjustment. For instance, in
[11] and [12], reconfigurable voltage quadrupler rectifiers are
proposed for applications requiring a wide output voltage

main drawback of these solutions is the large step in the output
voltage variation (twice or four times the base voltage), besides
the need for additional hardware, such as an active switch
and multiple capacitors. Similarly, a reconfigurable converter
capable of modifying the primary bridge operation and the

also has the drawback of using additional hardware (e.g., bidi-

are proposed based on modifications to the transformer struc-

number of devices in the path of the current (compromising
the efficiency) are remarkable drawbacks.
In this context, this brief proposes a novel LLC resonant
converter using a reconfigurable rectifier with an adjustable
transformer turns ratio, as shown in Fig. 1. Different from
previous solutions presented in the literature, the proposed
converter enables regulation of a tailored portion of the out-
put voltage by adjusting the transformer turns ratio through
the reconfigurable secondary-side rectifier. Unlike doubler
or quadrupler converters, the voltage step in the proposed
topology is not constrained to fixed values. Instead, it can
be tailored according to the application, enabling small steps
(e.g., 5-20%) suitable for SST systems, or larger steps such
as output voltage doubling for EV charging stations, which
typically require voltage regulation between 400-800 V. In
other words, the output voltage step is a design choice. Even
though the proposed converter introduces an additional switch
on the secondary side (denoted as S R,1 in Fig. 1), the overall
hardware remains simpler compared to other solutions, leading
to a reduced number of active components in the current path.

designed based on the LV mode. As the converter is designed
to operate as a DC transformer, the fs is defined slightly below

equation, it is found Cr = 0.7 nF.

V. CONCLUSION
This brief proposed a novel reconfigurable LLC resonant
converter using a center-tapped transformer and a secondary-
side reconfigurable rectifier. The converter delivers fixed
output voltage steps through a secondary-side active switch
while maintaining constant switching frequency. Experimental
results from a 1 kV / 4 kW prototype confirm the feasibility,
effective voltage regulation, and turns ratio adaptation of the
design. The converter also shows good dynamic performance
in the transition between the LV and HV modes and high
efficiency in both modes. Furthermore, the converter attains a
peak efficiency of 97.6% in HV mode at 4 kW. Despite the
extra losses from the active switch in the HV mode, the overall
semiconductor losses are reduced due to the lower current
stress on the secondary side.
