A Novel Error Metric for Evaluating the Error
Correction Capability of Approximate Units
Yuqin Dou, Haroon Waris, Roger Woods, Senior Member, IEEE and Weiqiang Liu, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—The use of Approximate Arithmetic Units (AAUs) in
an error-tolerant application enhance circuit performance and
reduce power consumption. Recently, a lot of technical work
is reported on the Approximate High-Level Synthesis (AHLS)
where these AAUs are used to create high-quality approximate
system-level designs, To enhance the quality of approximate
designs produced by AHLS the existing studies primarily focus
on search algorithms within AHLS, while neglecting the fact
that the optimal approximate designs exhibit a higher frequency
of effective error capability. Therefore, the objective of this
work is to delve into the error correction capabilities of AAUs
that have not received adequate attention and integrate them
in AHLS design. This effort provides new perspectives and
methods to improve the quality of AHLS design exploration. To
achieve this, we propose a novel metric for evaluating the error
correction capabilities of AAUs, detailing its calculation method
and properties. We applied this metric to two AHLS case studies
and experimentally validated its effectiveness as AHLS achieved
superior approximate designs.
Index Terms—Approximate computing, DSE, Number-aware
Pruning, GA

I. I NTRODUCTION
The approximate arithmetic units (AAUs) use an approximation design philosophy that allows controlled computational
errors to significantly reduce energy consumption and enhance
computation speed, optimizing computing resources while
maintaining system performance. The AAUs have already
been successfully applied in error-tolerant applications. For
example, [1] replaced the precise operations of a Gaussian
filter with an approximate multiplier, resulting in 32.5% power
savings. Additionally, [2] described the design of an approximate multiplier for machine learning, which improved the
energy efficiency by 1.7x (up to 2.1x) with negligible impact
on machine learning accuracy.

Recently, a new research focus has emerged on how to
combine different AAUs to minimize errors and maximize the
approximation benefits1 . The existing state-of-the-art approach
to address this challenge is Approximate High-Level Synthesis
(AHLS), which can automatically search for different combinations of AAUs and select an optimal combination for the
final approximate design. The research on AHLS primarily
focuses on improving outcomes through search algorithms or
error estimation; however, it overlooks the error correction capabilities of AAUs [3] [4] [5]. The error correction capability
of AAU refers to utilize its own inherent errors to correct
input errors. The inclusion of this feature can improve the
results of AHLS. In high-quality approximation designs, the
AAUs tend to have a higher frequency of triggering error
correction (Section V will introduce the relationship between
error correction capabilities and optimal approximate design.).
To better understand and utilize this capability, we propose
a new metric for evaluating the error correction capability of
AAUs. Moreover, we have systematically devised a calculation
method and properties of this metric. We applied the metric
to two AHLS case studies and experimentally validated its
effectiveness in helping AHLS achieve optimal designs. This
work not only provides a new perspective for approximate
design but also opens new avenues for optimizing efficient
computing systems in AHLS.
II. BACKGROUND
A. Approximate arithmetic unit
The AAUs optimize energy consumption, area, and speed
of the circuit on account of minimal accuracy loss.The current design methodologies for AAUs primarily include logic
simplification and voltage scaling. The voltage scaling technology achieves power reduction by lowering the voltage of
the circuit. However, this typically impacts the speed and
stability of the circuit, and the control over circuit accuracy
becomes challenging. Contrary, the logic simplification involves reducing the complexity and power consumption of
the circuit by simplifying the computational logic, such as by
merging certain logic gates or reducing the number of bits. The
advantage of this method is that it allows for direct control over
the source and magnitude of errors, facilitating precise adjustments to the performance and accuracy of the approximate
circuit. Therefore, modern research in the design of AAUs
predominantly focuses around the logic simplification. A large
number of open-source high-quality AAUs are now available

B. Approximate High-Level Synthesis
The emergence of numerous AAUs has greatly advanced
the field of AHLS. The AHLS aims to utilize approxima- tion
techniques to automatically generate designs that achieve high
approximation benefits while satisfying the error constraints.
In recent studies, [4] has used approximate units as core technology and combined space pruning and genetic algorithms
to improve the exploration quality of AHLS. [3] employed a
method based on prior knowledge to enhance design quality
while reducing the exploration time. [11] first constructed an
approximate library using approximate units and then applied
reinforcement learning to search for excellent approximate
designs. [12] utilized a jump algorithm to effectively search
the best designs and designed a metric to select approximate
units to ensure outstanding results. However, the method for
selecting approximate units in this work overlooked the error
correction capability between approximate units
In summary, these studies primarily focus on improving
algorithms to achieve better approximate designs, but they
neglect the fundamental reason for selectiong excellent approximate designs—research on the error correction capability
of approximate units. In this work, we proposed a a systematic
methodology that leverages AHLS to achieve better designs by
incorporating the error compensation mechanism of AAUs.

In this table, we can observe that the initial error of the
system is MED1 = 4.954. When AAUEcc is less than 0, the
approximate adder of a2 introduces errors into the system, as
its MED2 is bigger than MED1. Furthermore, given the same
initial error, the smaller the AAUEcc , the worse introduced
error. Conversely, in the last row, when AAUEcc is greater
than 0, the approximate adder of a2 improves the errors of
system, as MED2 is less than MED1. Moreover, we can see
that the variation of ’utilization’ aligns with AAUEcc . This
can be explained as follows; a larger AAUEcc indicates that
AAU have more errors that play a role in correcting the error of
system. Consequently, with the same approximate benefits, the
errors introduced into the system decrease. This can provide
us with an insight while selecting an appropriate approximate
adder from the set of available approximate adders to configure
node a2 . Typically, we would utilize the calculation method
of ”utilization” which involves calculating the error first and
then performing complex physical synthesis. However, we can
also use AAUEcc instead of ”utilization” at this point, thereby
avoiding the complex physical synthesis process.
In summary, we can conclude two properties of AAUEcc :
• The larger the value of AAUEcc , the smaller the error
introduced, and the more errors are corrected. When
AAUEcc is greater than 0, it indicates that the error
characteristics of the AAU can improve the error of
system.
• The value of AAUEcc also reflects the ’utilization’ of
the approximate unit. This implies that AAUEcc can be
used for the preliminary selection of approximate units,
allowing users to assess the ’utilization’ without complex
physical synthesis.

VI. A PPLICATION C ASE
A. Formation of the initial configurations
In ENAP [4], genetic algorithm(GA) is employed as the
search algorithm to obtain high-quality approximate designs.
However, the results of GA are constrained by the initial designs.In the original framework, ENAP lacked suitable criteria
for creating initial designs; thus, a random method was adopted
to create these initial designs. In this work, AAUEcc is utilized
to assist in the formation of initial designs, which is shown as
follows:
Step1: Initially, a node is selected at random, and an AAU
is configured randomly.
Step2: Starting from the node of the previously configured
AAU, the next step involves sequentially selecting the AAU
with the maximum AAUEcc from the approximation library.
Step3: Finally, the process concludes either when the configuration of all nodes is complete or when the error constraint
is exceeded, thus forming an initial design.

B. Selection for Approximate Units
[12] employed the jump search(JS) for the design space
exploration of approximate circuits. This method initially
configures the first node, and then sequentially configures
subsequent nodes by selecting approximate units from an
approximation library. The selection of these approximate
units is governed by the FOM(Figure of Merit) defined in
[12] . The FOM does not account for variations in error
propagation; it only selects the approximate unit that provides
the greatest area savings at that moment. Therefore, it does not
guarantee the selection of the most suitable approximate unit.
We modified the JS framework by adopting AAUEcc as the
criterion for selecting approximate units and the approximate
unit with the highest AAUEcc is selected for design each time.

for this experiment. It consists of 7 nodes, including 4 multiplication operations and 3 addition operations. The Fig. 4
